About Rainy Brain

Rainy Brain is a web application with a Machine Learning model that predicts whether it will rain the next day, based on the Rain Prediction in Australia dataset. The project's code is available in my GitHub repository. Over 80% of Australia receives less than 600 mm of annual rainfall, the lowest among all continents except Antarctica. For example, areas near Lake Eyre receive only about 81 mm yearly, while the Australian desert averages between 81 and 250 mm. Despite the low rainfall, thunderstorms are fairly common, with 15 to 20 annually. In southern Australia, westerly winds and rain-bearing cold fronts are typical in winter, causing frosts inland but mild temperatures near the coast. Summers are hot and dry with coastal breezes, but prolonged dry spells can lead to bushfires, especially in Victoria and New South Wales. Northern Australia experiences a wet season from October to April due to the monsoon, bringing showers, thunderstorms, and occasionally heavy rainfall from tropical cyclones, reaching further inland.
